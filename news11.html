Short-staffed Security Teams Rely on AI to Address Growing Threats

  • Benjamin Hartley
  • | November 13, 2025

Security leaders facing workforce shortages are turning to artificial intelligence to close the skills gaps in their organizations, according to survey results released today by a global cybersecurity company.

97% of 1,850 IT and cybersecurity decision-makers across 29 countries reported using or planning to use a cybersecurity solution that leverages AI to address a growing number of security incidents, according to the 2025 Fortinet Cybersecurity Skills Gap survey conducted by Sapio Research. The survey noted that the vast majority of its respondents had one or more security breaches in 2024. More than half of organizations surveyed say breaches cost them more than US$1 million.

In response to that growing problem, the study noted that organizations are increasingly turning to AI to strengthen their capabilities and posture, even as they acknowledge AI could also be used against them as an engine for new or improved cyberattacks.
