Google Android PC: Attempt at Redemption After Chrome OS Failures

  • Thomas Granger
  • October 29, 2025

During the Qualcomm Summit on September 25 2025, Google has announced they are partnering with Qualcomm to create a new operating system for computers, combining Android and Chrome OS to bring Android-based PCs to the mainstream. These Android-powered PCs are set to release next year.“I've seen it, it is incredible,” Qualcomm CEO Christiano Amon commented. “It delivers on the vision of convergence of mobile and PC. I can't wait to have one,” he added.

This is not Google's first attempt at scaling the world's dominant mobile operating system into the laptop form factor. We've seen it with Chrome OS that ran on Chromebooks, which unfortunately never really took off. The success of the new Android-powered PCs will depend on Google's ability to learn from past models that failed, including Microsoft's attempt at a unified OS with Windows 8 and the Windows Phone as well as Apple's ongoing plan of scaling up iOS to become more desktop-like, evident in their effots to optimise iPadOS for desktop-class productivity.

Qualcomm does not only make flagship processors for Android, but PC chips as well. They also announced the Snapdragon X2 Elite and Elite Extreme claiming they are the most efficient chips available for Windows PCs. There was no mention of Google, but we can speculate that these chips might be the ones powering the Android PCs when they eventually release, bringing ARM-based PC processors to a new heights.
