Alphabet Clears DOJ Review for $32 Billion Wiz Cybersecurity Acquisition

  • Helena Petrovic
  • | November 13, 2025

Google’s parent company, Alphabet, has reached a major milestone in its record‑breaking $32 billion acquisition of Wiz, a leading cloud security firm. The U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ) has officially cleared the antitrust review, signaling that regulators do not see the deal as a threat to competition in the cybersecurity market.

This acquisition is set to significantly strengthen Google Cloud’s security portfolio, integrating Wiz’s advanced threat detection and cloud protection tools into Google’s ecosystem. Wiz CEO Assaf Rappaport described the clearance as “an important step,” though international regulatory approvals are still pending before the deal can close in 2026.

The move underscores Google’s growing investment in cybersecurity as cloud adoption accelerates worldwide. By acquiring Wiz, Google aims to position itself as a leader in enterprise security, competing more aggressively with Microsoft and Amazon in the cloud services market.
