AMD Announces Multi-billion Dollar Deal with OpenAI: Stock Skyrockets

  • Rachel Whitmore
  • | October 26, 2025

AMD has announced a multibillion dollar deal with OpenAI, where AMD is providing 6 gigawatt of their Instinct GPUs to power OpenAI's foundational model training. The first 1 gigawatt deployment of AMD Instinct MI450 GPUs is set to begin in the second half of 2026. In return, OpenAI will take a stake in AMD worth up to 160 million shares, or roughly 10% of the company.

“We are thrilled to partner with OpenAI to deliver AI compute at massive scale,” said Dr. Lisa Su, CEO of AMD. “This partnership brings the best of AMD and OpenAI together to create a true win-win enabling the world's most ambitious AI buildout and advancing the entire AI ecosystem.” “This partnership is a major step in building the compute capacity needed to realize AI's full potential,” said Sam Altman, co-founder and CEO of OpenAI. “AMD's leadership in high-performance chips will enable us to accelerate progress and bring the benefits of advanced AI to everyone faster.”

For decades, AMD has been battling with two corporate giants. In the CPU market, it was struggling against Intel's dominance of the CPU manufacturing industry. In the GPU market, it has constantly rivaled Nvidia for a distant second place. Yet, under the leadership of CEO Lisa Su, AMD has undergone a remarkable transformation fueled by brilliant engineering and the hubris of its rivals.

AMD's stock has generally underperformed Nvidia's over the last year, rising just 1% in the past 12 months. Nvidia's stock price rose 52% during the same period. Things have picked up over the last six months, however, with AMD shares climbing 75%. "It will be tens of billions of dollars of revenue to AMD over the next few years," said Dr. Lisa Su during an interview with Yahoo Finance Executive Editor Brian Sozzi. "We see it ramping to double digit billions of revenue for us. It's incremental from an AMD standpoint."
