Google Threat Intelligence Group Warns of AI‑Powered Cyberattacks

  • Helena Petrovic
  • | November 13, 2025

Google’s Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG) has released a new report highlighting how adversaries are increasingly misusing artificial intelligence to enhance cyber operations. State‑sponsored actors from countries such as North Korea, Iran, and China have been observed experimenting with AI‑enabled tools to conduct reconnaissance, generate phishing lures, and exfiltrate sensitive data.

The report warns that AI‑powered malware can now generate malicious scripts and adapt its code on the fly to bypass detection systems. Attackers are also using generative AI to craft highly convincing social engineering campaigns, including phishing emails and voice‑cloning attacks that can trick employees into granting system access.

Google emphasizes the need for stronger defenses, including zero‑trust frameworks and AI‑driven security solutions, to counter this evolving threat landscape. The findings highlight the dual nature of AI: while it offers powerful tools for defenders, it also equips cybercriminals with unprecedented capabilities.
