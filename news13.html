Samsung Internet Arrives on Windows: The Future of AI Browsing

  • Anika Suresh
  • | November 13, 2025

Samsung has just launched a desktop version of its web browser, Samsung Internet. According to the company, Samsung Internet is "evolving from a PC browser that waits for input to an integrated AI platform", following the examples of OpenAI's ChatGPT Atlas, Microsoft's Edge Copilot and Opera's Neon.

Samsung Internet on Windows supports cross-platform syncing of data like bookmarks, browsing history and autofill. The company says it also prioritizes privacy and security, offering standard features like tracker blocking and a privacy dashboard. Samsung had previously launched their browser on Windows in 2024, before pulling it from the Microsoft Store. Contrary to that, this launch appears to be about positioning it in the rapidly emerging landscape of AI browsers.

Samsung says its cross-platform browser will advance its "vision for ambient AI," anticipating your needs and offering more personalized assistance.
