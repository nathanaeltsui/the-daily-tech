Apple Unleashes M5 Chip: 4x GPU Performance of Its Predecessor

  • Priya Desai
  • October 28, 2025

Apple has done it once again. They recently announced the upcoming M5 chip, that will debut alongside the new 14-inch MacBook Pro, iPad Pro, and Apple Vision Pro. Apple claims that "M5 delivers over 4x the peak GPU compute performance for AI compared to M4," and "up to 45% higher graphics performance than M4".

This is mainly thanks to the new architecture of the chip, built on the new third-generation 3nm process, with a new 10-core GPU having a Neural Accelerator in each core, allowing GPU-based AI workloads to run much faster. The chip also features an improved 16-core Neural Engine, and third- generation ray-tracing engine along with anincreased memory bandwith of 27.5% over its predecessor. All of this combined provides a significant jump in performance for AI and Machine Learning workflows.

The new 10-core CPU also comes with improvements, with Apple advertising a 15% faster multithreaded performance over M4. These numbers have been supported by leaked benchmarks, specifically a 10-12% increase in single-core performance and 15% increase in multi-core scores. The focus seems to be on the GPU performance rather than the CPU this time, where graphics benchmarks recorded up to 35% better performance.

Apple also also given examples of performance in terms of real-world use:

  • Time to first token (LLM): 3.6x faster than M4

  • Time to enhance video in Topaz Video: 1.8x faster than M4

  • Time to render in Blender with ray tracing: 1.7x faster than M4

  • Time to enhance speech with AI in Premiere Pro: 2.9x faster than M4

The power-efficient performance of M5 is going to help the new 14-inch MacBook Pro, iPad Pro, and Apple Vision Pro meet Apple's high standards for energy efficiency, and reduce product emissions as part of Apple's vision to be carbon neutral across its entire footprint.
