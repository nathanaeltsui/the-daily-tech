Institutional Investors Pour Billions into Small & Mid-Cap Tech Stocks in Singapore

  • Carmen Vega
  • | November 13, 2025

Singapore’s small and mid‑cap (SMID) stocks have seen a surge in institutional investment flows in the second half of 2025, reversing earlier outflows. These companies, valued between S$100 million and S$10 billion, now account for about 30% of the market’s total capitalization.

Data shows that SMIDs recorded S$472 million in net institutional inflows up to October 30, with sectors outside real estate investment trusts experiencing nearly S$890 million in inflows. This marks a strong rebound from the S$150 million outflow observed in the first half of the year.

Tech-focused SMIDs, including firms in electronics and digital services, are leading the charge. Analysts suggest this reflects renewed confidence in Singapore’s innovation ecosystem and the potential for smaller tech firms to deliver high growth in 2026 and beyond.
